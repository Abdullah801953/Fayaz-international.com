About Belarus

Belarus is officially known as the Republic of Belarus. Belarus is often referred to as white Russia or White Ruthenia. It is a landlocked country in eastern Europe. Russia borders Belarus to the east and northeast, Ukraine to the south, Poland to the west, and Lithuania and Latvia to the northwest. The current president of the country is Alexander Lukashenko. The country has many rare plants and animal species. Belarus is famous for its beauty and greenery. Belarus declared independence on 25 August 1991. In 2000, Belarus and Russia signed a treaty for greater cooperation, forming the Union State. Much of the borders of Belarus took their modern shape in 1939 when some lands of the Second Polish Republic were reintegrated into it after the Soviet invasion of Poland and were finalized after World War II. Belarus covers 207,600 square kilometers (80,200 sq mi), with a population of 9.4 million, and is the thirteenth-largest and the twentieth-most populous country in Europe. The country is administratively divided into seven regions and is one of the world's most urbanized, with over 40% of its total land area forested. Minsk is Belarus's capital and largest city. Belarus National flag is a red-and-green flag with a white-and-red ornament pattern placed at the hoist end. The current design was introduced in 2012 by the State Committee for Standardisation of the Republic of Belarus and adapted from a design approved in a May 1995 referendum. There is no official interpretation for the colors of the flag but according to an explanation given by President Alexander Lukashenko is that red represents freedom and the sacrifice of the nation's forefathers, while green represents life. Belarus climate is a temperate-continental climate with moderate features, which is characteristic of Central Europe, with hot summers, long, cold winters, and very distinct seasons. Daytime temperatures vary from -5-0°C in the winter and 25-30°C in the summer months. The country is also famous for its sweets or desserts.

About Minsk the Capital City

Minsk is the capital and the largest city of Belarus, located on the Svislač and the Niamiha rivers. Minsk is the administrative capital of the Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) and the seat of its Executive Secretary. As of January 2018, its population was 1,982,444, making it the 11th most populous city in Europe. Minsk is in the area of mixed forests typical of most of Belarus. Pinewood and mixed forests border the edge of the town, especially in the north and east. Some of the forests were preserved as parks as the city grew. Its weather is unstable and tends to change relatively often. The average January temperature is −4.5 °C (23.9 °F), while the average July temperature is 18.5 °C (65.3 °F). The lowest temperature was recorded on 17 January 1940, at −40 °C (−40 °F), and the warmest on 8 August 2015 at 35.8 °C (96 °F). Fog is frequent, especially in the autumn and spring.

About Gomel

Gomel is a city in southeastern Belarus. There are many stories about the origin of the name, but the most famous is that the name is derived from the name of the stream Homeyuk, which flowed into the river Sozh near the hill where the first settlement was founded. Gomel was founded at the end of the 1st millennium AD on the lands of the Eastern Slavic tribal union of Radimichs. It lies on the banks of the Sozh river and the Homeyuk stream. Sozh's high right bank, cut through by canyons, provided a natural fortification. For some time, Gomel was the Gomel Principality's capital, before it became part of the Principality of Chernigov. Gomel is the Gomel Region's administrative center and, with 526,872 inhabitants (2015 census),[2] the second-most populous city of Belarus. The coldest month in Gomel, Belarus, is January, with an average high-temperature of -2°C (28.4°F) and an average low-temperature of -6.9°C (19.6°F). The Ferris Wheel and the Ferris Tower, located in a park a few hundred meters from the palace complex, are famous for exploring the city. Since Gomel's topography is relatively flat, the height of the surrounding buildings means it is easy to view the city from the wheel and tower.
