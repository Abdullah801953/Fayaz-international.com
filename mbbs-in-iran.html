About Iran

Iran, the Islamic republic, is the Middle East's easternmost country. The country is bordered on the north by the Caspian Sea and the south by the Persian Gulf and the Gulf of Oman. Persia was the previous name for the country. Armenia, Turkey, Iraq, Afghanistan, Azerbaijan, Pakistan, and Turkmenistan border it on land, while Kazakhstan and Russia, in the Caspian Sea, Kuwait, Qatar, Bahrain, Oman, the United Arab Emirates, and Saudi Arabia have sea boundaries.

The country's population is estimated to be around 83 million people (as of 2020 data). Tehran, the republic's capital and political and economic hub, is the country's largest metropolis. Iran was first united by the Iranian Medes in the seventh century BC and reached its territorial apex in the sixth century BC when Cyrus the Great formed the Achaemenid Empire, which became one of the world's first superpowers and one of the greatest empires in history.

Iran is a regional and intermediate power in Asia, having a geopolitically significant location. It is a founding member of the United Nations, the World Economic Organization, the Organization of Islamic Cooperation, and the Organization of Petroleum Exporting Countries. It possesses huge fossil fuel reserves, including the second-largest natural gas supply and the fourth-largest proven oil reserves in the world. The 22 UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the country showcase the country's rich cultural heritage.

Eligibility Criteria for MBBS In Iran
To be able to apply for MBBS in Iran, students must meet all of the following requirements:

  • They must be 17 years old on or before December 31st of the admittance year.

  • They must have received 50% in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology in their 12th grade from CBSE / ICSE or an equivalent board of examination.

  • They must have passed the NEET(UG) Entrance exam (as per MCI's recent notice) to apply for MBBS in Iran.
